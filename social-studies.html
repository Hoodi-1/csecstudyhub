Course Overview
What You Need to Know
Social Studies develops the knowledge, skills and attitudes for effective social participation in the Caribbean community and wider world.

🎯 Aims of the Syllabus

  • ▸ Develop appreciation for self as active, responsible members of society
  • ▸ Encourage respect for differences in opinion, lifestyle and cultural practices
  • ▸ Develop social and life skills for social interaction
  • ▸ Appreciate the environment and contribute to its sustainability
  • ▸ Adapt to changing social, economic, political and technological forces

🔗 The 8 Unifying Concepts

All content is organised around these core concepts. Expect to reference them in your SBA and essay responses.

🧭
Identity
Who am I? What do I want to be? Personal and group belonging.
🗽
Freedom / Choice
The freedom to adapt, reject norms, and be creative.
⚔️
Conflict
Inherent in exercising freedom among competing alternatives.
🌱
Development / Change
Results of choices at personal, national, and regional levels.
🤝
Interaction
Relationships among people at all levels of society.
🌐
Cooperation
Sharing ideas and resources to achieve goals.
⚖️
Justice
Fairness; maintaining social, economic, legal and moral rights.
🔄
Integration
Policies achieving national and regional goals through shared rules.

Section A
Individual, Family & Society
Covers family structures, social groups, institutions, government, and electoral processes in the Caribbean.
Paper 01: 30 multiple-choice items from Section A.  |  Paper 02: 2 structured/short-answer questions (both mandatory).
1. The Family — Concepts & TypesSO 1–3▶

Key Terms to Know:

  • Kinship, status, nuclear family, extended family, sibling household, single-parent family
  • Monogamy, polyandry, polygyny, bigamy, incest
  • Patriarchal / matriarchal; patrifocal / matrifocal; patrilineal / matrilineal
  • Marriage, legal separation, divorce, annulment, alimony, sibling, inheritance

Types of Unions: Visiting relationships, consensual/common law, monogamy, legal marriage

Functions of the Family: Unit of procreation; socialisation of the individual; economic unit for basic needs; emotional and psychological support

2. Roles, Responsibilities & Changing RolesSO 4–5▶

Understand the roles and responsibilities of adult and sibling members of Caribbean families and the factors driving change:

  • Status of women in society
  • Choice of employment and educational opportunities
  • Available technology and leisure activities

Results of changing roles: Identity crisis, marginalisation of the male, role conflict, development of women's self-esteem and independence.

3. Parenthood, Parenting & Social IssuesSO 6–9▶

Preparation for parenthood: Physical, economic, emotional and psychological readiness.

Characteristics of a good parent: Problem-solving skills, communication, socialising skills, disciplinary skills, nutrition knowledge, love/warmth, money management.

Contemporary Social Issues:

  • Teenage pregnancy, street children, substance abuse
  • Juvenile delinquency, STIs, child abuse, poverty
  • Domestic violence, trafficking in persons, suicide, incest
  • Care of the aged and those with special needs

Family Laws cover: Inheritance, child care, legal separation, divorce, domestic violence.

4. Cultural Diversity of the CaribbeanSO 10▶
  • Cultural patterns, customs, ceremonies, religions, festivals
  • Promotion of ancestral customs, art, craft, language, music, dance, folklore
  • National organisations and groups that preserve cultural heritage
5. Social Groups & InstitutionsSO 11–16▶

Key terms: Primary, secondary, formal, informal, peer, interest, voluntary/involuntary membership, culture, institution, social control, folkways, norms, mores, laws

Characteristics of groups: Structure, common goals, voluntary/involuntary membership, marks of identity, common needs/values, rules, sanctions.

Group cohesion requires: Leadership, control, cooperation, authority, commitment, loyalty.

Types of Interaction: Competition, conflict, cooperation, compromise.

Functions of Institutions: Economic, Educational, Recreational, Religious, Political.

6. Electoral Processes & GovernmentSO 17–23▶

Electoral Key Terms: Government, political party, constituency, candidate, electorate, voters' list, floating voter, adult suffrage, franchise, manifesto, ballot, hung parliament, coalition government.

Electoral Systems:

  • First-Past-The-Post: Winner takes constituency seat; may not reflect overall popular vote
  • Proportional Representation (PR): Seats allocated proportional to votes received

Structure of Government (Separation of Powers):

  • Legislature: Makes laws; includes Opposition and Head of State roles
  • Executive: Implements laws; includes Civil Service
  • Judiciary: Magistrates' Courts, High Courts, Privy Council, Caribbean Court of Justice

Functions of Government: Raise revenue; provide social services; manage finances; maintain law and order; create employment; maintain international relations; defend the nation.

7. Citizens, Constitution & Good GovernanceSO 24–25▶

Key Terms: Constitution, democracy, authoritarian, Ombudsman, human rights

Characteristics of Good Governance: Participation, independence of the judiciary, transparency, accountability, responsiveness to citizens, access to information, prudent use of resources, efficient civil service, freedom of expression, consultation.

Section B
Sustainable Development & Use of Resources
Part (i) covers human and natural resources; Part (ii) covers regional integration and CARICOM.
Paper 02 — Section B: Two mandatory questions — one on Development & Resources (short answers), one on Regional Integration (extended essay).
Part (i): Development & Use of Resources
Key Concepts — Human & Physical ResourcesSO 1▶

Human Resource Concepts:

  • Population, birth rate, death rate, natural increase, life expectancy
  • Infant mortality rate, fertility rate, migration, immigration, emigration
  • Net migration, brain drain, urbanisation, depopulation
  • Population density/distribution, dependency ratio, labour force
  • Employment, unemployment, underemployment, unemployable

Physical Resource Concepts:

  • Development, environment, conservation, energy
  • Renewable resource, non-renewable resource, natural resource
  • Infrastructure, food security, pollution, global warming, greenhouse effect
  • Environmental impact assessment, sustainable development
Population — Structure, Distribution & ChangeSO 2–5▶

Population characteristics: Age, sex, occupation, ethnicity, religion, dependency ratio.

Factors influencing distribution: Relief of land, climate, fertile areas, mineral resource location, developed areas, vegetation.

Population Change Factors: Birth rate, death rate, migration.

Sources of Population Statistics: National census, Registrar's Office, Office of Naturalisation, religious institution records.

Uses: Planning for housing, health care, education, employment, social welfare, infrastructure.

Migration — Causes & ConsequencesSO 6▶

Push Factors: Unemployment, poverty, lack of services, political instability, natural disasters.

Pull Factors: Better employment, higher wages, better education/health, political stability.

Types: Rural-urban, urban-rural, within Caribbean, Caribbean to rest of world.

Consequences: Impact on the individual, host country, and country of origin (brain drain, remittances, cultural exchange).

Human Resource Development & EmploymentSO 7–10▶

Development of Human Resources through: Nutrition and health; access to primary and curative health care; education (basic, moral, vocational, professional); scholarships; sports; culture.

Factors influencing employment:

  • Availability and use of capital
  • Level and range of skills demanded vs available
  • Availability and creation of markets
  • Level of technology in resource development

Job Selection Process: Collect job information → List options → Assess options → Determine consequences → Obtain resources → Know rights and responsibilities.

Natural Resources — Location, Use & ConservationSO 11–12▶

Renewable Resources: Agricultural land, water, forests, beaches, marine life, solar energy, wind.

Non-Renewable Resources: Petroleum, bauxite, gold, diamond, other minerals, geothermal energy.

Proper practices: Conservation, sustainable harvesting, food security measures.

Improper practices & effects: Over-cropping, deforestation, pollution, global warming, food shortages.

Part (ii): Regional Integration
Challenges Facing the Caribbean RegionSO 2▶
  • Small size and small national markets
  • Lack of diversification; low production and productivity
  • Unemployment and underemployment
  • Differences in resource distribution
  • High levels of indebtedness; high cost of imports
  • Shortage of skilled workers; inadequate technology
  • Low value of exports; difficulties accessing developed markets
  • Shortage of capital; vulnerability to natural disasters
Stages of the Integration MovementSO 3▶

Know the sequence and membership of each stage:

  • West Indies Federation — first attempt at political union
  • CARIFTA — Caribbean Free Trade Association
  • CARICOM — Caribbean Community
  • OECS — Organisation of Eastern Caribbean States
  • ACS — Association of Caribbean States
  • CSME — CARICOM Single Market and Economy
OECS, CARICOM & CSME — ObjectivesSO 4–5▶

OECS and CARICOM: Functional cooperation; integration of member economies; harmonisation of foreign policy.

CARICOM Single Market: Free movement of goods and services, capital, and skilled labour.

CARICOM Single Economy (Harmonisation of):

  • Fiscal (tax) policies
  • Monetary policies (including a single CARICOM currency)
  • External trade policy and legislation
  • Sectoral policies in agriculture, manufacturing, fisheries

Regional Development Fund assists disadvantaged members.

Factors Promoting & Hindering IntegrationSO 6–7▶

Promoting: Common cultural heritage; common economic and social issues; effects of globalisation; vulnerability to economic shocks and natural disasters.

Hindering: Geography of region; no common development model; differences in stages of development; competition for industries; insularity; absence of common currency; unequal resources; influence of multinational corporations.

Benefits of Integration & Regional AgenciesSO 8–10▶

Benefits: Reduced unemployment; better response to globalisation; improved quality of life; free movement of goods, labour and capital; increased market size; improved international competitiveness; expansion of trade.

Key Regional Agencies:

  • CARDI (Agriculture), WICB, CONCACAF (Sports)
  • CEHI, CAREC (Health), CEDERA (Emergency Response)
  • CMC (Media), UWI, CXC (Education)
  • CCJ (Justice), RSS (Security)

📡 C1 — Communication
Forms & Media of CommunicationSO 1–3▶

Key Terms: Communication, communiqué, news agency, medium, message, receiver, sender, transmission, journalism, propaganda, censorship, freedom of expression, libel, slander, Internet, copyright, plagiarism.

Forms of Communication:

  • Non-verbal: Pictograms, screams, signals, signs, gestures, postures, mime, art forms
  • Verbal: Speech, print, song, drama

Media: Mail, telegraph, radio, television, print, satellite, facsimile, computers, cell phones, Internet.

Factors influencing media choice: Geographical (distance, climate), socio-cultural (language, status, beliefs), technological (electronics, energy).

Breakdown in Communication & Mass MediaSO 4–7▶

Causes of Breakdown: Age and gender differences, prejudices, beliefs, ideology, status, unresolved conflict, mistrust, stress, failure of equipment.

Forms of Media Ownership:

  • Private — individuals, groups, companies, corporations
  • Public — Government corporations
  • Joint — public-private partnership

Regional mass media and integration: Radio/TV stations, printing establishments, regional news agencies, departments of information.

Challenges of global media: Cultural penetration, effects on identity, attitudes, behaviour, creativity, nationalism.

Cultural Heritage, Copyright & TechnologySO 8–11▶

Transmitting cultural heritage through: Cultural groups, artists, mass media, institutions.

Copyright protection: Copyright laws, institutions for preservation of creative work.

Regulatory practices: Licensing, mediating disputes, censorship boards, code of ethics.

Communications Technology & Integration: Cellular phones, Internet, electronic transfers, email, telecommunications networks.

🛒 C2 — Consumer Affairs
Consumers, Demand & Modern TransactionsSO 1–4▶

Key Terms: Consumer, budgeting, saving, consumption, goods, services, demand, supply, income, disposable income, investment, marketing, credit union, hire purchase, e-commerce, exchange rate, devaluation, inflation.

Categories of Consumers: Individuals, groups, institutions; consumers of goods, services, and credit.

Factors influencing demand: Income, taste patterns, savings, credit, fluctuations in supply, quality, pricing, advertising, consumer expectations.

Modern Transactions: Tele-banking, ATM, E-commerce, Internet banking, debit/credit cards. Benefits: security, convenience, time-saving, 24-hour access.

Consumer Protection, Thrift & SavingSO 5–9▶

Government protection: Price control, quality control (Bureau of Standards), Fair Trading Commission, consumer affairs division, food and drug inspection.

Ways to practise thrift: Shopping wisely, care of possessions, conservation and recycling, budgeting.

Benefits of credit unions: Lower interest on loans, dividends on shares, financial counselling, easier access to loans, patronage refund, self-discipline, sense of ownership.

Inflation, Globalisation & Sustainable DevelopmentSO 10–12▶

Effects of devaluation/inflation: Higher prices, reduced purchasing power, lower standard of living, retrenchment.

Strategies to deal with effects: Home gardening, self-employment, budgeting, thrift.

Effects of globalisation on consumers: Cheaper goods, wider choice, more technology use, competition from developed countries.

Consumers and sustainable development: Prefer local/regional goods; invest in local businesses; conserve resources; consume products that promote healthy lifestyles; save to provide investment capital.

🌴 C3 — Tourism
Tourism Types, Products & Factors of DevelopmentSO 1–3▶

Key Terms: Tourism (international, regional, domestic), excursionist, cruise passenger, tourism sector (direct, indirect), economic linkage, economic leakage, nature tourism, sports tourism, health tourism, heritage tourism, all-inclusive, timeshare.

Tourism Products: Sand, sea, sun; nature/eco; health; sports; cultural/heritage; music festivals; special events.

Factors in HOST country: Accessibility; availability and quality of services; safety/security perception; political stability; infrastructure.

Factors in SUPPLY country: Disposable income; effectiveness of promotion; marketing; accessibility; cost of transportation.

Economic Contribution & Careers in TourismSO 4–5▶

Economic Contribution: Foreign exchange earnings; direct employment (hotel workers); indirect employment (agriculture, craft, transportation); government revenue (taxes, departure tax, licenses).

Career Types: Managerial, clerical, administrative, professional, technical, unskilled.

Conditions of employment: Trade union membership, job security, promotion opportunities, fringe benefits.

Environmental & Socio-Cultural ImpactsSO 6–7▶

Physical Environmental Impact: Resort development, changes to ecology and reefs, land reclamation, sewage disposal, water pollution, conservation of flora/fauna/historical sites.

Socio-Cultural Impact: Effects on land ownership, visitors' perceptions, citizen-visitor relationships, cultural exchange, influence on lifestyle/language/dress/values, government policies on beaches and immigration.

Government Policies, Agencies & ChallengesSO 8–11▶

Government Tourism Policies: Infrastructure; trained workforce; environmental concerns; crime; investment climate; overseas marketing presence.

Key Agencies: Hotel Associations, Tourist Boards, Caribbean Tourism Organisation, Caribbean Hotel Association, Hospitality Training Institutions, Caribbean Development Bank.

Challenges: Marketing, research, training, labour shortage, water supply, global terrorism, lack of direct air access, emerging destinations, cruise ship policies.

Using Tourism for Integration: Market Caribbean as single destination; common economic and marketing policies; aviation hubs; CARICOM passports.

School-Based Assessment
SBA Research Project Guide
Your SBA is a single guided research project worth 40 marks (20% of your total grade). Follow these steps carefully.
Word Limit: Do not exceed 1,000 words (excluding charts, graphs, tables, and pictures). A penalty of 10% applies if you exceed the limit by more than 150 words.

📋 The 9 Required Tasks

1

State the Problem

Write your research topic clearly in the form of a question (e.g., "What are the effects of teenage pregnancy on family life in…?")

2

Give Reasons for Selecting the Area of Research

Explain why this topic is relevant and important to your community or society.

3

Select an Appropriate Method of Investigation

Choose from: questionnaire, interview schedule, observation checklist, or documentary search. Justify your choice.

4

Design a Simple Data Collection Instrument

Write out your exact questionnaire questions or interview schedule. Ensure questions address relevant variables.

5

Describe Procedures for Data Collection

State how you chose your sample — number of persons, their gender, age group, area of residence, and how you collected data from them.

6

Present Data Using at Least THREE Forms

Use a combination of graphs, charts, tables, maps, diagrams, photographs AND prose. All must be appropriately labelled and accurate.

7

Analyse and Interpret Data

In light of your research question, explain what the data means. Connect your findings to Social Studies concepts.

8

State Findings

Write three clear statements about what you discovered from your investigation.

9

Make Recommendations

Provide two recommendations based on your findings and explain how you would implement at least one of them.

📦 Presentation Requirements

  • ▸ Submit in a simple soft-backed folder (Quarto or A4 size)
  • ▸ Write name, registration number, title, and school name on the cover AND first page
  • ▸ Include a table of contents at the beginning
  • ▸ Keep presentation neat, legible, arranged in task order
  • ▸ Place all illustrations appropriately in the body of text

⚠️ Authenticity Rules

All work must be your own. Teachers will check authenticity by:

  • ▸ Discussing the project with you directly
  • ▸ Asking you to explain your research methods and summarise findings
  • ▸ Having you complete aspects of the project in class
  • ▸ Monitoring the project throughout its development
