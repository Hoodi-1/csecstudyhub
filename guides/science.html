Biology
The study of living organisms, life processes, and the continuity of life — from cellular structure to ecosystems and genetics.
SEC A
Living Organisms in the Environment
7 General Objectives · 7 Topic Areas
▼

An introduction to the diversity and complexity of living organisms and their relationships with the environment. Students explore habitats, food webs, energy flow, and the impact of human activities on ecosystems.

General Objectives
  • Recognise diversity and similarity of form in living organisms
  • Understand the importance of the abiotic environment to living organisms
  • Appreciate interdependence between organisms and their environment
  • Understand energy flow through organisms within the ecosystem
  • Appreciate recycling of materials in nature and finite world resources
  • Be aware of the effect of human activities on the environment
  • Apply knowledge to identify problems affecting growth and survival of populations

SEC B
Life Processes and Disease
10 General Objectives · 10 Topic Areas
▼

Life processes are largely illustrated in humans and flowering plants. The focus is on the interdependence of internal processes at the organ and cellular level in maintaining a healthy organism, alongside diseases common in the Caribbean.

General Objectives
  • Know the structure of unspecialised cells and functions of main cell structures
  • Understand nutrition — how organisms obtain energy and material requirements
  • Understand respiration as the means by which energy is made available
  • Understand the role of transport, storage and defence in living organisms
  • Understand processes by which organisms excrete and regulate body fluids
  • Understand mechanisms of movement and its roles in living organisms
  • Understand detection and response to changes in internal/external environment
  • Understand how organisms increase in mass, size, and complexity
  • Understand processes by which life is perpetuated (reproduction)
  • Appreciate the social and economic importance of disease control

🔬
SBA Lab Skills
Master Manipulation & Measurement (MM), Observation, Recording & Reporting (ORR), Analysis & Interpretation (AI), and Planning & Design (PD) skills. These are assessed throughout your School-Based Assessment.
🧬
Drawing Diagrams
Practise drawing and labelling biological diagrams — cells, organ systems, flowers, chromosomes. Use a sharp pencil, label with straight lines, and always include a title and magnification.
🌏
Caribbean Context
The syllabus emphasises Caribbean ecosystems, diseases (HIV/AIDS, Dengue), and environmental issues. Know local examples for habitats, food webs, and human impacts on the environment.

Chemistry
The study of matter — its properties, composition, reactions, and energy changes. From atomic structure to organic compounds and metal chemistry.
SEC A
Principles of Chemistry
12 General Objectives · 9 Topic Areas
▼

Introduces fundamental chemical concepts: the particulate nature of matter, atomic structure, bonding, properties of elements and compounds, and quantitative chemistry. Forms the core conceptual foundation for all other sections.

General Objectives
  • Be aware that matter is made up of particles
  • Understand that different mixtures can be separated based on component properties
  • Be familiar with the atom as the basic building block of matter
  • Appreciate that matter can be classified based on physical or chemical properties
  • Be aware of different forces of attraction that exist between particles
  • Understand the mole as the unit for comparing amounts of matter
  • Appreciate that properties of chemicals affect their reactions
  • Assess the impact of certain materials on living systems and the environment
  • Understand the composition of certain materials and make informed choices about their use
  • Understand that rate of chemical reaction depends on physical factors
  • Appreciate that energy changes occur during chemical reactions
  • Appreciate the importance of chemistry to the environment

🧮
Mole Calculations
Master the mole formula: n = m/M and n = V/22.4 (at STP). Practise reacting mass calculations, titrations, and percentage composition — these appear frequently in Paper 2.
🔥
Equation Balancing
Always balance chemical equations. Use state symbols (s), (l), (g), (aq). Ionic equations and half-equations for electrolysis are commonly tested — practise these thoroughly.
🧪
Lab Practicals
Know the tests for cations, anions, and gases (H₂, O₂, CO₂, NH₃, Cl₂). The SBA emphasises experimental design, recording observations, and drawing valid conclusions from data.

📐
Show Your Working
In calculation questions, always write the formula first, substitute values with units, then solve. Even if your final answer is wrong, you can earn marks for the correct method.
📊
Graph Drawing
Use a ruler, label both axes with quantities and units, choose an appropriate scale, and draw the best-fit line (not dot-to-dot). Gradient calculations are a key skill for Paper 2.
🔌
Electricity Focus
Section D is the most heavily weighted section. Practise circuit diagrams, Ohm's Law problems, and power calculations. Transformer and logic gate questions are also commonly tested.
