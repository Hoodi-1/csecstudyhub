Purpose of the Syllabus

The CSEC Religious Education syllabus helps students explore the meaning and purpose of life through the lens of four world religions and Caribbean Indigenous Religions. It fosters respect, understanding, and critical thinking in a multi-cultural Caribbean society.

Key Aims

  • Develop understanding of the meaning and purpose of life as advanced by different religions
  • Encourage informed dialogue among cultural and religious groups
  • Promote critical and reflective thinking about religious beliefs
  • Foster appreciation and respect for diverse belief systems
  • Create awareness of religious heritage as Caribbean people

🗝️ Key Structure Every candidate studies the Compulsory Core (Paper 01) plus ONE Option (Paper 02). The Core covers Essentials of Religion + 4 World Religions + Caribbean Indigenous Religions.

Option A — Christianity (Deeper Study)

Option
▾
📋 Option A Sections Section 1: Human Life Issues (36 marks — compulsory) | Section 2: The Bible (32 marks) | Section 3: God (32 marks) | Section 4: Sin and Salvation (32 marks)

Biblical Teachings on Meaning & Purpose of Life: Humans as creatures made in God's image (Gen 1:26–27); endowed with intelligence and free will; God as source of all life and values.

Concept of Stewardship: Co-workers with God; care for the sick, suffering, child, and outcast.

Life Issues to Know:

Child AbuseSubstance AbuseCapital Punishment PovertyUnemploymentPrejudice & Discrimination Gender RolesViolence & WarHIV/AIDS

Concept of the Reign of God: OT — Israel as God's special care (Exod 19); NT — Paul at Athens (Acts 17:22–31); New Heaven and Earth (Rev 21:1–3).

Transmission & Preservation: Dead Sea Scrolls; formation of the Canon; Old and New Testament books.

Types of Writing in the Bible:

Narrative (Pentateuch, Acts) Laws / Exhortations (Matt 5–7; Ex 20) Prophecy (Amos, Hosea, Isaiah) Apocalyptic (Rev, Daniel) Parable (Matt 13) Wisdom Sayings (Proverbs, Eccl) Miracle Stories (Mark, Luke, John)

Biblical Guidelines for Living: Ten Commandments; Sermon on the Mount; Golden Rule (Matt 7:12); Greatest Commandment (Matt 22:36–40); Christ's New Commandment (John 13:34–35); Christian Love (1 Cor 13).

Roles of God: Creator, Provider, Protector/Liberator, Sovereign Ruler, Source & Sustainer of Life, Revealer & Teacher.

Names of God: Yahweh, Adonai (Lord/Master), Elohin (Mighty One), El-Shaddai (Almighty), Jehovah-Jireh (Provider), Jehovah-Rapha (Healer), Jehovah-Shalom (Peace), Christos, Yeshua (Salvation), Father/Advocate/Lord.

The Trinity: God as Father, Son (Jesus/Lamb of God/Bread of Life), and Holy Spirit (Advocate, present at Creation).

Attributes of God: Unity · Eternal · Omnipresent · Omniscient · Omnipotent · Love · Justice · Holiness · Righteousness · Mercy.

Concept of Sin: State/condition of humankind; transgression & rebellion; violation of God's law; universal phenomenon; personal & corporate failure.

Consequences of Sin: Alienation from God & others; suffering/degeneration; guilt/shame; death.

Concept of Salvation: Deliverance, Freedom, Liberation from bondage of sin.

Process of Salvation:

  1. Repentance
  2. Justification by Faith
  3. Forgiveness / Reconciliation / Atonement
  4. Regeneration / New Birth
  5. Sanctification (growing into Godlikeness)

Option B — Hinduism (Deeper Study)

Option
▾
📋 Option B Sections Section 1: Human Life Issues (36 marks — compulsory) | Section 2: The Ramayan/Bhagvad Geeta (32 marks) | Section 3: The Absolute and Avatars (32 marks) | Section 4: Sin and Liberation (32 marks)

Meaning & Purpose of Life: One's lifetime is an opportunity to move 'God-wards', ending the cycle of re-incarnation (Uttarkaanda Ramayan Dhoaa 44).

Guruhood & Discipleship: The Guru guides devotees to realise their divine nature (e.g. Ekalavya; Mahatma Gandhi).

Sanctity of Life: Life is one and sacred — illustrated by Rama's attitude to Guhaa (boatman), Jataayu (bird), Shabari (tribal devotee).

Origins: Valmiki Ramayan originally in Sanskrit, rewritten in Hindi by Tulsi Das. The Geeta was imparted by Shri Krishna to Arjuna who could not fulfil his duty as a warrior.

Literary Styles: Prose; Poetry — Chowpai / Dohaas / Shloka / Sorthaa / Chhanda.

Core Guidelines: "Promoting the welfare of all is one's highest duty"; "Distressing another is the greatest sin."

Key Examples from Scriptures: Honesty (Gandhi – Gita 16:1–3); Righteousness (Bharat); Faith (Shabari); Forgiveness (Jayant); Mercy (Shri Rama to Rawana); Faithfulness (Lakshman & Bharat).

God as All: "Twameva Maataa Cha Pitaa Twameva" — You alone are Mother, Father, Brethren, Companion, Friend, Knowledge, Wealth and God All.

Names of God: Vishnu (All-Prevailing, Sustainer); Brahma (Creator); Mahesh (Dissolver/Destroyer).

Nirgun vs Sagun Brahma:
Nirgun Brahma = unmanifest, absolute, eternal, no physical qualities.
Sagun Brahma = manifest with form, relative, with physical qualities.

Concept of Sin: Transgression of divine law; no inherent/original sin; no mortal sin resulting in eternal hell; atonement through Tapsya (austerity/penance).

Consequences of Sin: Alienation from God and man; suffering and spiritual death; unhealthy environment and disease.

Moksha (Liberation): To be liberated, the individual must be one with God in all thoughts, feelings, words and actions.

From Bondage to Liberation: Example of Valmiki — changed from highway robber to Saint.

Option C — Islam (Deeper Study)

Option
▾
📋 Option C Sections Section 1: Human Life Issues (36 marks — compulsory) | Section 2: The Holy Qur'an (32 marks) | Section 3: Concept of Allah (32 marks) | Section 4: Sin, Punishment and Reward (32 marks)

Purpose of Life: Knowing/worshipping and attaining closeness to God (Qur'an 51:56); Man as Allah's representative on earth (Qur'an 2:30); Life as a sacred trust (Qur'an 33:72).

Khilaafah (Vicegerency): Humans are Allah's stewards on earth; common parentage (Adam and Eve); distinction based on piety and righteousness; equality of all people before God.

Ibaadah (Worship): Pervasive — includes social ethics, moral behaviour, social responsibilities, Salaah (prayer), and Zikr (constant remembrance of God).

Socio-Economic Solutions: Zakaah (compulsory charity); charity for the destitute; interest-free banking; protection from exploitation of labour.

Preservation: First revealed to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him); Allah Himself preserves the Qur'an (Qur'an 15:10); transmitted orally and in writing; compiled under early caliphs.

The Arabic Language: Only Arabic can convey the full profound meaning; translations are only interpretations of meaning.

Miraculous Nature: Permanent challenge to those who doubt it; literary form and expression; archaeological, historical and scientific evidence.

The Hadith: Second source of guidance alongside the Qur'an; records sayings, actions and approvals of the Prophet; classified as Sahih (authentic), Hasan (sound), Daif (weak).

Tawheed (Oneness of God) — Three Aspects:

  • Tawheed al Rububiyyah — Unity of Lordship
  • Tawheed al Ulluhiyyah / Ibaadah — Unity of Worship
  • Tawheed al Asma wa al Sifaat — Unity of His Names and Attributes

Belief in Angels: Nature of angels (Qur'an 13:13; 35:1); roles; recording angels (Qur'an 50:17–18).

Concept of Prophethood; Hereafter; Al-Qadr (Divine Decree).

Option D — Judaism (Deeper Study)

Option
▾
📋 Option D Sections Section 1: Human Life Issues (36 marks — compulsory) | Section 2: The Tenakh (32 marks) | Section 3: God (32 marks) | Section 4: Festivals and Observances (32 marks)

Topics include: meaning and purpose of life from a Jewish perspective; Jewish teachings on justice, peace, human dignity; roles and responsibilities within family and work; application of Jewish ethical principles to contemporary Caribbean life issues.

Study all 8 Jewish festivals in detail (see Core Section 5 above for full descriptions): Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, Pesach/Passover, Shavuoth, Sukkoth, Simchath Torah, Purim, Chanukkah.

Focus on: date/timing, religious significance, rituals performed, key symbols and how each festival connects to Jewish history and scripture.
