Examination Overview

The EDPM examination is offered at General Proficiency only. Your final grade is split between an External Assessment (60%) and School-Based Assessment (40%).

Paper 01  · 1h 30min
Multiple Choice 24%
60 items from all syllabus sections.
Tests Accuracy & Speed (12 marks) and Knowledge & Comprehension (48 marks).
Paper 02  · 2 hours
Practical Exam 36%
4 questions / 100 marks. Includes a 10-min speed test (Q1). Tests copy-typing, letters, manuscripts, tables, reports, forms & creative displays.
Paper 03/1  · SBA
School-Based Assessment 40%
3 assignments (75 marks) + Portfolio (25 marks). Tests all three profile dimensions across Terms III–V.

Profile Dimensions

Every piece of work is assessed against three profiles. Know what each one demands.

⚡
Accuracy & Speed
Professionally completed documents using correct language (spelling, punctuation, grammar, abbreviations) within a specified time period.
🧠
Knowledge & Comprehension
Ability to list procedures, identify facts, state principles, and critically analyse situations related to EDPM.
🖥️
Presentation & Use of Technology
Produce a finished product to professional standards (formatting, style consistency) and demonstrate competency with technology (e-filing, retrieval).
I
Section I
Fundamentals of Computing
  • 1
    Define the computer
    An electronic machine/device that accepts and processes data to produce information.
  • 2
    Identify types of computers
    Mainframe, Minicomputer, Microcomputers — including laptop, desktop, tablet, palmtop and other current devices.
  • 3
    Advantages and disadvantages of computer usage
    Consider: speed, accuracy, storage, and reliability.
  • 4
    Input devices
    Keyboard, touchscreen, mouse, light pen, microphone, document scanner, OCR/OMR readers, barcode readers.
  • 5
    Output devices
    Printers (impact: dot matrix, line; non-impact: laser, inkjet, page); monitors, speakers, multimedia projector.
  • 6
    Storage media
    CD-ROM, DVD, Floppy Disk, Hard Disk, CD-RW, Flash drives, Memory cards.
  • 7
    Operating System vs Application Software
    OS: Coordinates computer device activities, allows apps to run.
    Application: Performs specific user tasks — word processing, spreadsheet, database, presentation, anti-virus.
  • 8
    Caring for computers and peripherals
    Proper cleaning, handling, storage, and maintenance of hardware devices in the work environment.
  • 9
    Health and safety in computer use
    Health/Ergonomics: Effects of extended use, inadequate lighting, inappropriate furniture, bad posture.
    Safety: Overloading sockets, workstation positioning, extension cord placement.

II
Section II
Keyboard Mastery
🎯
Target Speed
You must achieve a minimum typing speed of 35 words per minute (wpm) incorporating all keyboarding skills.
  • 1
    Touch keyboarding techniques
    Practice and develop speed and accuracy on alphanumeric keys using correct touch technique.
  • 2
    Correct posture at the computer
    Correct posture, arm reach, seat height, screen-to-eye-level contact.
  • 3
    Special keys, shortcut keys, and function keys
    Special: Tab, Escape, Insert. Shortcuts: Ctrl, Alt combinations. Function keys: F1–F12.
  • 4
    Spacing after punctuation marks
    Rules for spacing after: full stop, comma, semicolon, colon, question mark, exclamation mark, apostrophe, parenthesis, quotation marks.
  • 5
    Manuscript signs (proofreading marks)
    Interpret and apply standard correction/proofreading marks as listed in the syllabus appendix.
  • 6
    Types of headings
    Main head, sub-head, shoulder, paragraph, marginal, and others — with appropriate use of each.
  • 7
    Typescript from manuscript or typed notes
    Rules for paragraphing (blocked, indented, hanging), inset paragraphs, abbreviations, typing of figures and ellipsis.
  • 8
    Language skills for professional documents
    Correct punctuation, grammar, spelling, and knowledge of abbreviations.
  • 9
    Type at a minimum of 35 wpm
    Incorporating all skills from objectives 1–8.
Key Terms
Touch Typing 35 WPM Manuscript Signs Proofreading Posture Paragraph Styles Punctuation Rules

Section III
Introduction to Application Software
4 Core Software Types
  • Word Processing — text documents
  • Spreadsheet — numeric data
  • Database Management — data storage & retrieval
  • Presentation — lectures, speeches, reports
When to Use Each
  • Word processing: letters, reports, manuscripts
  • Spreadsheet: calculations, charts, invoices
  • Database: storing tables, queries
  • Presentation: visual slide-based delivery
  • 1–5
    Identify and explain each application type
    Know the purpose, key features, and appropriate use cases for Word Processing, Spreadsheet, Database, and Presentation software.
  • 6
    Select appropriate software for specific tasks
    Given a scenario, determine whether to use word processing, spreadsheet, database management, presentation, or other specialised software.

IV
Section IV
Use of Application Software
  • 1
    Formatting features
    Font types, styles, sizes, colour, bold/underline/italics. Line spacing, alignment, bullets & numbering, margins, symbols, borders & shading, background, watermarks, endnotes, footnotes, headers, footers, page numbering.
  • 2
    Editing techniques
    Proofreading; cut, copy, paste, move; search & find/replace; add & delete; spelling & grammar check; thesaurus; type-over vs insert mode.
  • 3
    Tabulations
    Simple: column & row headings, oblique/vertical headings, borders, table alignment, sorting.
    Advanced: Formulae (Sum, Average, Min, Max), charts/graphs, % and $ formatting, invoices, debit notes, credit notes.
  • 4
    Create a database using a single table
    Create and populate a database with no more than 5 fields; perform a simple query using one or two fields.
  • 5
    Simple presentations
    Title slides, transitions, timing, appropriate fonts, animations, layouts, graphics, and effective communication.
  • 6
    Integrate information across applications
    Mail merge (using an external data source), embedding objects from different applications.
💡
Exam Tip — Tabulations
Know the difference between simple and advanced tabulations. Advanced tabulations often appear in Paper 02 with formula instructions. Practice Sum, Average, Min, and Max functions.

Advanced Displays

Section VII
Electronic Communication
  • 1
    Types of electronic communication
    Telecommuting, Email, Internet/World Wide Web, social media (blogs, podcasts, forums), social networking (Facebook, Twitter), facsimile, multifunctional devices, scan-to-mail, scan-to-file.
  • 2
    Features of e-mail
    To, CC (carbon copy), BCC (blind carbon copy). Sending attachments.
  • 3
    Advantages and disadvantages of electronic communication
    Speed of delivery, cost, ability to send to multiple recipients simultaneously.
  • 4
    New and emerging communication technologies
    Compare usage and features: Twitter, Facebook, smartphones, blogging.
  • 5
    File organisation using e-mail
    Storing and sorting mail by date, sender, and subject. Contact list organisation.
  • 6
    Factors for selecting communication media
    Degree of urgency, genre (oral, written, visual), level of confidentiality, location/time zone, cost, efficiency, effectiveness.

Document Management
  • 1
    Manual vs Electronic filing systems
    Definitions, comparisons, advantages and disadvantages of each system.
  • 2
    Types of documents in data processing
    Source documents, turnaround documents, and machine-readable documents.
  • 3
    Manipulate an electronic filing system
    Organisation of drives, folders, and files in a hierarchical (tree) structure.

IX
Section IX
Ethics
  • 1
    Intellectual property
    Protection of rights of persons who produce, gather or disseminate ideas and information — authors, singers, journalists, etc.
  • 2
    Rights protected by intellectual property laws
    Economic rights: Payment for use of the product by third parties.
    Moral rights: Protection against misrepresentation or misquoting of the work.
  • 3
    The copyright concept
    Producers/Owners: Maintain ownership and receive financial reward.
    Publishers/Distributors: Record, film, or print material; distribute it protecting producers' interests.
    Consumers/Users: Must acknowledge source or seek permission; may pay a fee.
  • 4
    Plagiarism
    Using someone's intellectual property without giving appropriate credit.
  • 5
    Guidelines to avoid plagiarism
    Acknowledgement of references in text and bibliography (using referencing formats). Obtaining approval for others' work.
  • 6
    Planning and ordering priorities
    Organisation of work and workstation; submission deadlines; effective time management; follow-up procedures.
  • 7
    Desirable habits and work attitudes
    Individual responsibility, willingness, meeting deadlines, team spirit, co-operation, recognition of diversity, courtesy, punctuality & regularity, good grooming, personal integrity, respect for others.
Key Terms
Intellectual Property Copyright Plagiarism Economic Rights Moral Rights Work Ethics Time Management

School-Based Assessment (SBA)

SBA accounts for 40% of your final grade. It is made up of three assignments (75 marks) and a portfolio (25 marks).

Assignment 1 — Term III
Copy-typing from manuscript 25
Correct text from author's original work with amendments. Apply printers' correction signs. Covers Sections I–IV.
Assignment 2 — Term IV
Electronic Research 25
Conduct research, present findings electronically, prepare for dissemination via e-mail with bibliography. Covers Sections IV–VI and IX.
Assignment 3 — Term V
Project-Based Creative Work 25
Design documents (flyers, menus, invitations, newsletters) using various applications. Covers Sections III, IV, VI and IX.

Accuracy Marking (5 marks)
  • Correct spelling throughout (2+ errors = 0)
  • Appropriate punctuation and line-ending hyphenation
  • Copy free of transposition errors, mis-strokes, omitted/added words
  • Correct capitalisation
  • Information communicated logically with correct grammar, abbreviations, figures, paragraphing, referencing

Key Glossary Terms

Essential terms from the EDPM syllabus you must know for Paper 01.

Computer
An electronic machine that accepts and processes data to produce information.
Hardware
The physical components of a computer system (e.g., keyboard, monitor, CPU).
Software
Programs and operating information used by a computer — includes OS and application software.
OCR / OMR
Optical Character Recognition / Optical Mark Recognition — character reader input devices.
Mail Merge
Combining a document template with a data source to produce personalised copies.
Metadata
Data stored about a document: date created/modified/accessed, owner, access privileges.
Versioning
Tracking successive document versions that change over time.
Encryption
Converting data into a coded form to prevent unauthorised access.
Ergonomics
The study of designing equipment and environments for human comfort, efficiency, and safety.
Plagiarism
Using someone's intellectual property without giving appropriate credit.
Copyright
Legal protection of the rights of producers/owners of creative works.
Telecommuting
Working from a remote location using electronic communication tools.
Turnaround Document
A document produced by a computer, sent to a user, and returned for further processing.
Archiving
Long-term storage of documents no longer in active use but retained for legal or historical reasons.
Blocked Style
Letter/document format where all lines begin at the left margin.
Facsimile (Fax)
Electronic transmission of scanned documents over telephone networks.
