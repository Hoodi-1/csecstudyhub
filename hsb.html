Living Organisms & the Environment

Cells · Photosynthesis · Food Webs · Carbon Cycle

Cell Biology & Specialisation

+

Characteristics of Living Organisms

  • Nutrition, Respiration, Excretion, Growth, Irritability, Movement, Reproduction (MRS NERG)

Cell Structures & Functions

  • Nucleus — controls cell activities; contains DNA
  • Cell membrane — selectively permeable; controls what enters/exits
  • Mitochondria — site of aerobic respiration / ATP production
  • Chloroplasts — site of photosynthesis (plant cells only)
  • Cell wall — rigid cellulose structure (plant cells only)
  • Ribosomes — site of protein synthesis
  • Vacuoles — storage; large in plant cells, small in animal cells
  • Endoplasmic reticulum — transport network within cell

Specialised Cells to Know

  • Epithelial, sperm, egg, nerve (neurone), muscle, connective tissue cells
  • Cells → Tissues → Organs → Organ Systems

Microbes (for diagrams)

  • Virus, Bacteria, Fungi — know basic labelled structures
Exam tip: Be ready to label diagrams of plant vs. animal cells and state differences (e.g. cell wall, chloroplasts, large vacuole in plant only).

Transport: Diffusion, Osmosis & Active Transport

+
Simple Diffusion
Movement of particles from high → low concentration. Passive (no energy).
Osmosis
Diffusion of water through a semi-permeable membrane from dilute → concentrated solution.
Active Transport
Movement against a concentration gradient. Requires ATP energy.
  • Examples in plants: water uptake by root hair cells (osmosis); mineral absorption (active transport)
  • Examples in animals: glucose absorption in intestine (active transport)

Photosynthesis

+
  • Site: Chloroplast (chlorophyll absorbs light)
  • Word equation: Carbon dioxide + Water → Glucose + Oxygen (using light)
  • Chemical: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
  • Products: Glucose (used for energy, stored as starch) and Oxygen (released)
  • Light and dark reactions — not required for CSEC
Practical: Simple starch test on variegated and non-variegated leaves to investigate the role of light and chlorophyll.

Food Chains, Food Webs & Energy Flow

+
Producer
Plants; make food via photosynthesis.
Primary Consumer
Herbivore; eats producers.
Secondary Consumer
Carnivore; eats primary consumers.
Trophic Level
Position in food chain; energy decreases at each level.
Bioaccumulation
Build-up of toxins (e.g. pesticides) through trophic levels — effects on human health.
  • Know pyramids of numbers AND biomass — analysis required
  • Habitats: terrestrial, marine and freshwater aquatic

Carbon Cycle & Global Warming

+
  • CO₂ absorbed by plants in photosynthesis → converted to carbohydrates
  • CO₂ released by: respiration, decomposition, combustion, decay
  • Fossil fuels formed from ancient organisms; burning releases stored CO₂
  • Bacterial and fungal decomposition releases CO₂ from dead organisms
  • Greenhouse effect: excess CO₂ traps heat → global warming
  • Impact of global warming: rising sea levels, extreme weather, food security threats

Life Processes

Nutrition · Respiration · Circulation · Skeletal · Excretion · Nervous · Reproductive

B1 — Nutrition

+

Nutrients

Macronutrients
Carbohydrates, Lipids, Proteins — needed in large amounts.
Micronutrients
Vitamins (A, B1, C, D, E, K) and Minerals (Ca, Mg, F, I, P, Na, Fe) — small amounts.
Fat-soluble vitamins
A, D, E, K — stored in body fat.
Water-soluble vitamins
B, C — not stored; needed regularly.

Deficiency Diseases

Night blindness
Vitamin A deficiency
Rickets
Vitamin D / calcium deficiency
Goitre
Iodine deficiency
Anaemia
Iron deficiency

Malnutrition & BMI

  • Kwashiorkor (protein deficiency) vs Marasmus (energy + protein deficiency)
  • Obesity, anorexia, bulimia — definitions and health consequences
  • BMI = Weight(kg) / Height(m)² — also assess waist circumference
  • Balanced diet depends on age, sex, and occupation

Digestion

  • Mechanical digestion (mastication) and Chemical digestion (enzymes)
  • Key enzymes: amylase (starch→glucose), protease (protein→amino acids), lipase (fat→fatty acids + glycerol)
  • Absorption occurs in the small intestine via villi — know the structure of a villus
  • Glucose and amino acids → hepatic portal vein → liver; fats → lymphatic system
  • Role of water as solvent and transport medium
  • Dietary fibre — prevents constipation; link to obesity

Teeth

  • Types: incisors, canines, premolars, molars — roles in digestion
  • Tooth structure: enamel, dentine, pulp cavity, cement
  • Tooth decay: bacteria + sugar → acid → demineralisation
  • Care: brush twice daily, floss, fluoridation, bi-annual dental check-ups
Practical: Food tests — Benedict's (reducing sugars), Iodine (starch), Biuret (protein), ethanol emulsion (fat), and enzyme activity experiments (amylase/catalase at different temperatures & pH).

B2 — The Respiratory System

+
  • Breathing mechanism: diaphragm and intercostal muscles change chest volume → pressure changes → air moves in/out
  • Gaseous exchange in alveoli: large moist surface area, thin walls, copious blood supply
  • Aerobic respiration: C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP (lots of energy)
  • Anaerobic respiration (in humans): Glucose → Lactic acid + small ATP; creates oxygen debt
  • ADP + Pᵢ → ATP (energy stored); ATP → ADP + Pᵢ (energy released)
  • Vital capacity = maximum air exhaled after maximum inhalation
  • Factors affecting breathing rate: exercise, smoking, anxiety, drugs, altitude, obesity
  • CPR technique — know steps including disposal device for mouth-to-mouth
Smoking effects: nicotine addiction, lung damage (emphysema), carcinogens, reduced O₂-carrying capacity, hypertension, heart disease. Applies to cigarettes, marijuana, vaping, hookah.

B3 — The Circulatory System

+

Blood Components

Red blood cells
Biconcave; carry O₂ via haemoglobin; no nucleus.
White blood cells
Phagocytes (engulf pathogens) & lymphocytes (produce antibodies).
Platelets
Clotting; with fibrinogen → fibrin clot.
Plasma vs Serum
Plasma = fluid with clotting factors; Serum = plasma without clotting factors.

Blood Vessels

Arteries
Thick walls, narrow lumen, no valves; carry blood away from heart.
Veins
Thin walls, wide lumen, have valves; carry blood to heart.
Capillaries
One cell thick; site of exchange with tissues.

Heart & Circulation

  • 4 chambers: right/left atria and right/left ventricles
  • Left ventricle has thicker wall (pumps to whole body — systemic circulation)
  • Pulmonary circulation: heart → lungs → heart
  • Systolic pressure (heart contracting) vs Diastolic (heart relaxing)
  • Modifiable risk factors for hypertension: salt, fat, alcohol, smoking, stress
  • Blood groups: A, B, AB, O — antigens and antibodies; Rh factor in pregnancy
  • Clotting cascade: platelets → thromboplastin → prothrombin → thrombin → fibrinogen → fibrin
  • Heart diseases: atherosclerosis, coronary thrombosis, hypertension

Lymphatic System

  • Tissue fluid → lymph → lymph vessels → lymph nodes (filter pathogens) → back to blood

B4 — Skeletal System & Movement

+
  • Functions of skeleton: movement, locomotion, protection, support, breathing, blood cell production, mineral storage
  • Key bones: cranium, vertebral column, rib cage, sternum, pelvic girdle, humerus, radius, ulna, femur, tibia, fibula, patella
  • Bone vs Cartilage: bone is hard/mineralised; cartilage is flexible/at joints
  • Tendons connect muscle to bone; Ligaments connect bone to bone

Joints

Hinge joint
Elbow, knee — flexion and extension only.
Ball & socket
Hip, shoulder — wide range of movement.
Fixed joint
Skull sutures — no movement.
Synovial joint
Has synovial fluid for lubrication.

Muscles

  • Skeletal (voluntary), Smooth (involuntary, organs), Cardiac (involuntary, heart)
  • Antagonistic muscles: biceps (flexion) and triceps (extension) of the upper arm
  • Muscle tone — maintained by exercise; factors that adversely affect skeletal system: poor posture, inappropriate footwear, heavy lifting

B5 — Excretion & Homeostasis

+
  • Excretion: removal of metabolic waste (≠ egestion which is removal of undigested food)
  • Organs of excretion: Lungs (CO₂), Skin (sweat/salts), Kidneys (urea in urine)
  • Kidney: ultrafiltration in glomerulus → selective reabsorption in nephron → urine
  • Renal dialysis — briefly understand purpose and process
  • Skin layers: epidermis, dermis, adipose tissue; sweat glands, erector muscles, hair follicles, capillaries

Homeostasis & Feedback

Homeostasis
Maintenance of a stable internal environment.
Negative feedback
Corrects deviation from the set point (most common).
Positive feedback
Amplifies change from the set point.
  • Blood sugar: Insulin (lowers glucose) and Glucagon (raises glucose) from pancreas
  • Water regulation: ADH from pituitary — increases water reabsorption in kidney
  • Temperature regulation: vasodilation (cool down), vasoconstriction (warm up), sweating, shivering

B6 — Coordination & Control

+

Nervous System

  • CNS: Brain + Spinal Cord; Peripheral NS: spinal/cranial nerves + autonomic NS
  • Neurone types: sensory, relay, motor — know their roles
  • Synapse: chemical transmitters carry impulse across gap between neurones
  • Brain regions: cerebrum (thought), cerebellum (balance/coordination), medulla oblongata (breathing/heart rate), hypothalamus (homeostasis), pituitary (hormones)
  • Reflex arc: stimulus → receptor → sensory neurone → relay neurone → motor neurone → effector → response (involuntary, fast)

Eye

  • Know labelled internal structure; how images are formed (refraction by lens)
  • Accommodation: ciliary muscles and suspensory ligaments change lens shape
  • Defects: myopia (concave lens), hyperopia (convex lens), astigmatism, glaucoma, cataracts; link to diabetes

Endocrine System

Pituitary
ADH, FSH, LH, Growth hormone — "master gland"
Thyroid
Thyroxine — metabolic rate
Pancreas
Insulin & Glucagon — blood sugar
Adrenal
Adrenaline — fight or flight
Ovaries
Oestrogen & Progesterone
Testes
Testosterone

B7 — Reproductive System

+
  • Sexual reproduction: meiosis, gametes, variation; Asexual: mitosis, identical offspring
  • Know labelled diagrams of male and female reproductive systems
  • Related disorders: ovarian, cervical, prostate cancers

Menstrual Cycle & Hormones

  • FSH → follicle develops → Oestrogen → LH surge → ovulation → Progesterone → if no fertilisation, levels drop → menstruation
  • Ovulation → fertilisation → implantation → embryo development
  • Placenta functions: gas exchange, nutrient transfer, waste removal
  • Birth stages: dilation (stage 1), expulsion of fetus (stage 2), placenta expelled (stage 3); role of oxytocin

Family Planning & Contraception

  • Methods: natural, barrier, hormonal, surgical
  • Know advantages and disadvantages of each
  • Abortion: spontaneous (miscarriage) and induced; ethical considerations
  • Pre-natal/post-natal care: immunisation, balanced diet, breastfeeding benefits, avoiding drugs/alcohol/smoking

Heredity & Variation

DNA · Mitosis · Meiosis · Inheritance · Genetic Engineering

DNA, Chromosomes & Cell Division

+
DNA
Deoxyribonucleic acid — carries genetic instructions.
Gene
Segment of DNA coding for a specific protein/trait.
Chromosome
Coiled DNA; humans have 46 (23 pairs).
Allele
Alternative forms of a gene at a specific locus.
Diploid (2n)
Two sets of chromosomes (46 in humans).
Haploid (n)
One set of chromosomes (23 in gametes).

Mitosis vs Meiosis

Mitosis
Produces 2 identical diploid daughter cells. Purpose: growth, repair, asexual reproduction.
Meiosis
Produces 4 haploid cells (gametes). Introduces variation via crossing over.

Genetics & Inheritance

+
Dominant
Expressed when one or two copies present (capital letter, e.g. T).
Recessive
Only expressed when two copies present (lowercase, e.g. t).
Homozygous
Two identical alleles (TT or tt).
Heterozygous
Two different alleles (Tt).
Genotype
Genetic makeup (e.g. Tt).
Phenotype
Observable trait (e.g. tongue roller).

Monohybrid Inheritance Examples

  • Albinism and tongue rolling — use Punnett squares for crosses
  • Expected ratios: 3:1 phenotypic (Aa × Aa); 1:2:1 genotypic

Sex-Linked Inheritance

  • Sex determined by X and Y chromosomes (XX = female, XY = male)
  • X-linked conditions: haemophilia and colour blindness — females are carriers, males are affected
  • Know genetic diagrams for these crosses
Exam tip: You must be able to draw and interpret Punnett squares and calculate genotypic and phenotypic ratios.

Variation, Mutations & Natural Selection

+
Continuous variation
Gradual range (height, weight) — mostly genetic + environmental.
Discontinuous variation
Distinct categories (blood type, tongue rolling) — mostly genetic.
Mutation
Permanent change in DNA; can cause Down Syndrome, Klinefelter's, Turner's, albinism.
  • Natural selection: antibiotic-resistant bacteria is the key example
  • Sickle cell anaemia: high prevalence in people of African descent — natural selection advantage against malaria

Genetic Engineering & GMOs

+
  • Definition: manipulation of an organism's genome using biotechnology
  • Recombinant DNA: inserting human insulin gene into bacteria (E. coli) using plasmids
  • GMOs: genetically modified organisms in food (e.g. pest-resistant crops) and medicine
  • Advantages: increased yield, disease-resistant crops, cheaper medicines
  • Disadvantages: unknown long-term effects, ethical concerns, biodiversity reduction

Diseases & Their Impact on Humans

Communicable · Non-communicable · Vectors · Immunity · Drugs

Classification of Disease

+
Communicable diseases
Caused by pathogens; spread person to person (STIs, fungal, vector-borne).
Non-communicable (NCDs)
Not spread between people; includes lifestyle diseases, nutritional deficiencies, inherited disorders, mental health.
Signs
Objective, measurable indicators (fever, rash) — observed by a clinician.
Symptoms
Subjective experiences reported by patient (pain, fatigue).
Epidemic
Disease outbreak in a region.
Pandemic
Worldwide disease outbreak.

Specific Diseases — Causes, Symptoms, Treatment, Prevention

+

Respiratory

Asthma Influenza Bronchitis
  • For each: causes, signs/symptoms, treatment modality, prevention, effect on respiratory tract

Gastrointestinal

Cholera Gastroenteritis
  • Caused by contaminated food/water; proper hygiene in food preparation is key prevention

Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)

Gonorrhoea Syphilis HPV Herpes Chlamydia HIV/AIDS
  • HIV: mode of action on immune system; antiretroviral drugs (with side effects); ethical considerations including discrimination
  • Effects of STIs (gonorrhoea, syphilis, herpes) on pregnant mother and fetus

Chronic/Lifestyle Diseases

Obesity Diabetes (Type I & II) Hypertension Coronary Heart Disease
  • Importance of diet and exercise in prevention and management

Cancers

  • Respiratory tract, cervical, breast, ovarian, uterine, colon, prostate
  • Causes, symptoms, treatment, risk reduction strategies

Vectors & Mosquito-Borne Diseases

+
  • Vector: organism that transmits a pathogen (mosquito, housefly, rat)
  • Know the life cycle of mosquito AND housefly (labelled diagrams required)
Malaria
Plasmodium parasite via Anopheles mosquito.
Dengue
Virus (strains I–IV) via Aedes mosquito.
Zika
Virus via Aedes mosquito; risks in pregnancy.
Chikungunya
Virus via Aedes mosquito; joint pain.

Vector Control Methods

  • Biological (natural predators), chemical (pesticides), mechanical (nets, screens), sanitary (removing stagnant water)

Immunity, Sterilisation & Drugs

+

Types of Immunity

Natural active
Body produces antibodies after infection.
Natural passive
Antibodies passed from mother to baby (breast milk/placenta).
Artificial active
Vaccination — stimulates antibody production.
Artificial passive
Injection of ready-made antibodies/antiserum.

Microorganism Control

  • Sterilisation methods: UHT, pasteurisation, autoclaving, boiling, canning
  • Disinfectants (chlorine, bleach) used on surfaces; Antiseptics used on skin — know the difference
  • Antibiotics kill/inhibit bacteria — know definition of antigen, antibody, antitoxin

Drugs

  • Classes: stimulants, depressants, hallucinogens, narcotics
  • Prescription drugs: opioids, sedatives, painkillers, medicinal marijuana
  • Non-prescription: cocaine, heroin, ecstasy, alcohol, marijuana
  • Physiological and psychological effects; dependence; social/economic impact on individual, family, community
E

Health Practices & the Environment

Pollution · Water · Sewage · Waste Management

Pollution

+
  • Pollutants: domestic, industrial (thermal, heavy metals, oil dumping), agricultural
  • Air pollution: combustion of fuels; water pollution: sewage disposal, agricultural runoff
  • Eutrophication: excess nutrients → algal bloom → oxygen depletion in water
  • Control methods: renewable energy, legal regulations, improved waste management
  • Link to global warming and food/land security (see Section A)

Water: Cycle, Purification & Testing

+
  • Water cycle: evaporation → condensation → precipitation → run-off → transpiration → filtration through limestone
  • Home purification: boiling, purification tablets, chlorine/bleach
  • Large-scale purification: screening → sedimentation → filtration → chlorination
  • Testing water for bacteria: agar plate method
  • Contaminated water → cholera, gastroenteritis; human activities degrade water supplies

Sewage Disposal & Solid Waste

+

Sewage Treatment

  • Methods: biological filter and activated sludge — microorganisms break down organic matter
  • Improper disposal: contamination, spread of disease, eutrophication

Solid Waste

  • Dump vs Landfill — know the distinction; landfills are engineered with liners and drainage
  • Domestic refuse methods: composting, separation, bio-diesel, collection, burning
  • Biodegradable (organic matter) vs Non-biodegradable (plastics, metals)
  • 3Rs: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle — know examples of recyclable materials
Caribbean context: Landfill management is a major issue in the region. Be ready to discuss impacts and importance of landfills specifically for Caribbean territories.

SBA Research Project — Key Requirements

+
  • Topic: current health-related OR environmental issue with socio-economic impacts
  • Max 1,000 words (excluding bibliography, charts, appendices); penalty for exceeding by 150+ words
  • Must present data in at least two forms (table, graph, chart, etc.)
  • Individual write-up even if data collected in groups of 3–5

Report Structure (in order)

  • Cover page (candidate number, subject, date)
  • Table of contents
  • Introduction — background/overview, problem statement, research objective
  • Methodology — data collection, instruments, limitations
  • Presentation of data (minimum 2 forms, correctly labelled)
  • Analysis and interpretation of data
  • Conclusion
  • Recommendations (at least 2)
  • Reflection
  • Bibliography
  • Appendices

Top Exam Tips

+
  • Always label diagrams fully — many marks are awarded for labelled structures
  • Learn word AND chemical equations for photosynthesis and respiration
  • Practise constructing AND interpreting tables, graphs, and charts — this appears in every section
  • Understand Caribbean-specific examples: dengue, chikungunya, Caribbean food groups, obesity trends in the region
  • For genetics questions: always show full workings with Punnett squares
  • Paper 02 includes authentic scenarios — apply your knowledge, don't just recall facts
  • Know the link between topics: e.g. smoking → respiratory system → circulatory system → cancer
  • For data analysis questions: describe the trend, give specific values, suggest an explanation
